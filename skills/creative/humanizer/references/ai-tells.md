# AI Writing Tells — English (30+)

감사 시 이 목록과 대조한다. 각 항목: 패턴 → 왜 AI 티가 나는가 → 고치는 법.

## 어휘 (Lexical)

1. **Filler verbs**: delve, leverage, foster, harness, navigate, unlock, elevate, empower, streamline, utilize → use plain verbs (use, build, help, handle).
2. **Inflated adjectives**: robust, seamless, vibrant, intricate, crucial, pivotal, comprehensive, profound, multifaceted → cut or specify.
3. **Cliché nouns**: tapestry, testament, realm, landscape, journey, ecosystem, paradigm, game-changer, treasure trove → name the actual thing.
4. **"X is a testament to Y"** → just state Y.
5. **"In the realm/landscape/world of X"** → "In X" or delete.
6. **"navigate the complexities of"** → "deal with" / "work through".

## 구조 (Structural)

7. **Tricolon addiction** — three-part parallel lists everywhere ("fast, powerful, and intuitive"). Vary to two or four, or rewrite as prose.
8. **"It's not just X, it's Y"** — the signature LLM antithesis. Rewrite directly.
9. **"Whether you're X or Y"** opener → drop the frame.
10. **Rule-of-three in paragraphs** — three sentences, three examples, three points. Break the rhythm.
11. **Perfectly uniform sentence length** — AI writes mid-length sentences evenly. Mix short and long. Use fragments.
12. **Excessive parallelism** — every bullet starts with the same verb form. Vary structure.
13. **Bullet-point reflex** — turning flowing ideas into lists. Prefer prose unless a list truly helps.

## 구두점 (Punctuation)

14. **Em-dash overuse** — multiple em-dashes per paragraph. Max 1; replace others with commas, periods, parentheses.
15. **Semicolon sprinkling** in casual text → split into sentences.
16. **Curly-quote / formal punctuation** in informal contexts.

## 상투구 (Phrasal clichés)

17. **"In today's fast-paced world"** / "In an era of" → delete.
18. **"Let's dive in" / "Let's explore" / "dive deep"** → delete.
19. **"It's worth noting that" / "It's important to note"** → just say it.
20. **"At the end of the day"** → delete.
21. **"Here's the thing:"** → delete or rewrite.
22. **"The bottom line is"** → state the line.
23. **"That said," / "With that in mind,"** as filler transitions.

## 전환·결론 (Transitions & conclusions)

24. **Mechanical transitions**: Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, In addition, Consequently → use sparingly; connect by logic instead.
25. **"In conclusion," / "Ultimately," / "To sum up,"** → cut; let the last point land.
26. **"Not only... but also..."** overuse.

## 톤 (Tone)

27. **Forced balance / both-sides hedging** — "However, on the other hand..." even when a stance is warranted. Take a position.
28. **Over-hedging**: "may", "might", "could potentially", "in many cases" stacked. Be direct.
29. **Empty enthusiasm**: "exciting", "powerful", "revolutionary" without specifics.
30. **Sycophantic openers**: "Great question!", "Absolutely!" → delete.
31. **Emoji section headers** (🚀 Features, 💡 Tips) in prose → remove unless platform calls for it.
32. **Nominalization** — "the implementation of the optimization" → "optimizing".
33. **Passive voice default** — "mistakes were made" → name the actor.
34. **Adjective pairs**: "clear and concise", "quick and easy", "simple and effective" → pick one or be specific.

## 빠른 체크 (Quick scan order)

em-dash count → "delve/leverage/robust/tapestry" grep → tricolon → "It's not just" → "In conclusion" → uniform sentence length → emoji headers.
